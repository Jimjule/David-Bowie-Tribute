David Bowie

The man who touched the lives of millions

Some facts about Bowie:

  • Born David Robert Bowie in Brixton, London on 8th January 1947.
  • One of his pupils is permenantly dilated after he was punched during a fight at school, over a girl.
  • George Underwood - the same person who punched him at school - remained a good friend of Bowie, even doing artwork for some of his earlier albums.
  • His first-ever release was Liza Jane/Louie Louie Go Home in June 1964, under the name of Davie Jones with The King-Bees.
  • He released his debut album, the self-titled David Bowie, in 1967 after playing in a host of pub and club bands.
  • Bowie's first hit in the UK - 1969's Space Oddity - was used by the BBC in its coverage of the moon landing.
  • Bowie's first US number one was his single Fame in 1975. It was co-written by John Lennon and features the former Beatle on backing vocals.
  • Model Twiggy features with him on the cover for his 1973 album Pin Ups.
  • It is estimated that Bowie sold around 140 million albums over his career.
  • In the 1986 film Labyrinth, Bowie plays Jareth The Goblin King.
  • Bowie appeared as Pontius Pilate in Martin Scorsese's The Last Temptation of Christ.
  • Bowie married his wife Iman in 1992, and their daughter Alexandria was born in 2000.
  • David Bowie died from liver cancer on 10th January 2016, just after his 69th birthday.
